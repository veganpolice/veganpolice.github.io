# Human User Guide for @veganpolice

## About this document

Human User Guides (HUGs) are a descriptive document about the author. The goal of a human user guide is to make working together and collaboration with colleagues easier, faster. This is the "how-to" for me. [Learn more about human user guides](https://team.githubapp.com/posts/33572).

## About me

### As a person
- I live in a mountain resort town called Whistler. I grew up in the nearby city of Vancouver, Canada. 
- When I'm not on the computer I'm in the mountains snowboarding, biking and climbing
- I like to think critically about the world and am inspired by tools and technology which disrupt the status quo 

### At GitHub
- I am a Technical Program Manager working on the Mac Cloud (Actions) project
- I joined GitHub in 2019 as a novice developer with the original GitHub Insights team, "Gitalytics" 
- Find me in #ski-and-snowboard and #social-impact

### What are some honest, unfiltered things about you?
- I _love_ to have fun, laugh and express myself
- I am not afraid of a good argument and enjoy challenging other's views
	
### What drives you nuts?
- Interrupting others and taking up the majority of speaking time
- Notifications going off in the background of our zoom call
- Meetings that could be documents

### What are your quirks?
- I love to celebrate. If you ask me, I might write a rap and perform it for the occasion
- I cut my own hair and with varying results. Feel free to comment on my "future" styles. 
		
### What are some things that people might misunderstand about you that you should clarify?
- If I'm teasing you, it's because I trust you and I like you
- If I seem super "stoked", I'm not faking it. I can be genuinely over-the-top sometimes

## About my colleagues

### What qualities do you particularly value in your colleagues?
- I like to get to know colleagues and do my best work with those who share stories from their personal lives.
- Humour is important. If you make me laugh, we're going to do great things!
- I appreciate transparency and value seeing not only your output but your process. It helps me learn and builds trust. 

### What do you wish colleagues didn’t do?
- Please don't schedule meetings just to share or receive work related information. Written communication is preferred.
- I wish colleagues didn't communicate everything in direct messages. Public communication is preferred. 
	
### How can people earn an extra gold star with you?
- Talk to me about non-work stuff or tell me a joke
- Two gold stars if you can make a good joke about me! I'm always up for laughing at myself 
- Give me blunt feedback about how I can do better work

## About my interactions

### How do you coach people to do their best work and develop their talents?
- I try to understand peoples motivations and develop the right incentives
- Regular, direct feedback

### What’s the best way to communicate with you?
- I prefer written communication for work stuff
- I like to video chat for casual conversations
	
### What’s the best way to convince you to do something?
- Give me a hard deadline and tell me its important to you. I'm good with pressure and like to help. 
- Make it presentable. I love presenting. 
	 
### How do you like to give feedback?
- Directly and in private.

### How do you like to get feedback?
- Directly and in private.
